YOU THE FOREST

You the forest move like skies, you the trees and
breeze are waking with
every
bird and sun and
you
the forest sometimes cold with your bare
neck
and
limbs
all twisted. You the forest falling
and
you the
forest with
your
windy spine. I
want
you felled.
Everything a mist, your finger
whispers
down against
the floor
where moss and creek all
trickle, where
mist
is
breaking stick and twig. You
breathe the forest in
the skies. Your
breathe
the
dirt between your toes.
Your
neck stripped of leaf
and thorn
and the
trees all
split and heaving.
